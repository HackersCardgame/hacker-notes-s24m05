Meadow Whisper
In reply to this message
Government contracting jobs don't quite respond to the same market forces as the private sector, so they're "recession-proof" in the sense that an economic recession doesn't necessarily compromise job security. However, federal agencies generally treat their contractors as disposable subscription services, like their Netflix account, and whenever their budgets take a hit, contractors are the first thing to go.

And federal agency budgets are subject to the whims of Congress and the White House, so their budgets take hits for any number of reasons: Recession, recovery from recession, Twitter tantrums, racism, gratuitous obstructionism, natural disasters, random news stories, a Congressman finding out that branded pens exist, etc.

And if an agency cuts a contractor's position, that contractor will either lose their job with no notice, or be randomly reassigned to whatever else comes up (and if they don't like it, they can leave).

So, sure, government contracting is arguably not sensitive to the one specific job security factor of economic recessions. But if job security is what you're looking for, gov contracting is not the place to find it.
