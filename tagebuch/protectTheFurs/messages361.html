Peppercorn
The two chip banks on the ends are static RAM driven by 74393 ripple counters on the address lines and are arranged as a ping-pong buffer. The clever thing is that you can burst data straight in to them across the the 16-bit ISA bus as full bus speed without any on-board intervention.
09:26
The onboard 8052 microcontroller would then whack the data out and unpack it from RAM in to the DACs at a regular sample rate. But the 8052 isn't nearly fast enough to do 30-40K samples per second so I got clever.
09:28
There's a bunch of combinatorial GAL logic on there which forms a state machine. Clock is run directly in to the GAL and then the GAL runs through a state machine sequence the effectively produces the direct decode logic that is able to manually trigger the latch loads and bus advances out of the RAM banks straight in to the DACs. The 8052 mainly keeps track of where the transfer progress is in the buffers in order to indicate if the bank can be swapped around and to know when to reset the counters and start reading from the head end again.
