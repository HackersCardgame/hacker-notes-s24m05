01:51
There's a handful of different vendors, the biggest difference between vendors being amount of information cleanliness vs. availability. The Vera and Wink hubs are very clean but provide highly sanitized information, with a specific set of use cases in mind.
01:53
The same can be said for the other HA standard, ZigBee. The new Wink hub, as well as Phillips' Hue system are both fairly sanitized but have fairly dense data availability, but tend to leak information to their home base (Phillips logs a lot of information about lights, hubs, switches, etc). Other systems are relatively clean and provide things only in a rigid sense, such as IKEA's Tradfri system, which has only its internal systems in mind but has a lot of different configuration options within them since it was meant as a means to do HA without any external services.
01:55
There's some good options on both sides, with things like Zigbee2MQTT offering a high information density and low cost MQTT bridge in the more traditional sense, and there are a handful of good Z-Wave systems which are low cost comparatively and provide the higher info density. The downside to information density is that the software you're using to interact with it has more moving parts now, as it has to think about the actual things that those things are doing.
01:57
