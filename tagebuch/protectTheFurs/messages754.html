Salmonella is a manageable risk.

It's not inside the egg, it's on the egg shell, so:

1) don't wash your eggs, and if they are unwashed by the store (European eggs or some farmers) don't refrigerate.
2) if your eggs are washed, don't let them get condensation (leaving carton unrefrigerated for much time.
3) in either case, immediately after touching egg shell, wash your hands. Think of it like just raw meat.

If you follow those rules, your salmonella risk is next to non-existent. (1 and 2 keep the salmonella from crossing the egg shell barrier, btw)
