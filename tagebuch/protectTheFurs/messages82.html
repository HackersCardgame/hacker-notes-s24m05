Well, sadly, part of what you want to know about potential employers is stuff that you might not be able to find out through researching the company (though you may be able to filter out some employers by finding reviews produced by former employees). You may have to ask some questions in your in-person interview to see whether the company you're looking at has some of the same issues as your current employer.

Some example questions:

"What's your development workflow? Do you use a VCS? A dev server? Automated testing? If so, what kind(s) of these things do you use?"

"What metrics is employee performance measured by? What metrics is manager performance measured by?"

"What will my responsibility be with respect to designing the systems that the company uses? How is this responsibility expected to change as I gain more experience, and how will the requisite experience for such changes in that responsibility be measured?"

Note that these questions are designed with the intention of seeking out information about the work culture in ways that are hard to "buzz word" around. While an interviewer might have the insight to notice that you're looking for certain work culture traits, they're likely to either not know what answer you're looking for (employee/manager metrics) or not be able to pass as representing a good work environment without blatantly lying (VCS/dev environment/automated testing).
