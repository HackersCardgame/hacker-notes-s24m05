*fidgets* also... I'm currently assessing things for bit flips... so... uh... I have to deal with radiation mitigation techniques in my FPGAs
04:29
One of the most fun examples of mitigation issues is in the bog-standard moving average filter. It's usually implemented as a pair of adders (or adder and subtractor, if you want), an accumulator and a FIFO.

Each time a sample comes in, it's value is added to the accumulator and pushed into the fifo. You then pop a value from the other end of the FIFO and subtract it from the accumulator. The value of the accumulator is all of the samples within the FIFO added together so you divide it by the length to get the average
04:31
Okay... so... if an SEU (single event upset - a.k.a. bit flip) happens on the input or output, all fine as the incorrect value will make it's way through the filter and will self-clear as soon as it's out...

But what if you have an SEU in the FIFO... well, then you add one value and subtract a different one as it exits. This means that your moving average filter now has an offset and will have that offset until a power cycle, reset or enough more SEUs happen that it somehow balances out or gets worse...
04:31
There's no way to recover without just throwing everything out within the FIFO and zeroing the accumulator
